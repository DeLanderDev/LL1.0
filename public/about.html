About Local Lee

A neighborly network for the people of Lee County, Illinois.

What this is

Local Lee is a small, slow, locally minded website. It does four things:

  • It keeps a directory of locally owned businesses in Lee County - farms, shops, trades, eateries, and the rest. Owners can claim and update their own listing.
  • It runs a community calendar of events anyone in the county can submit, and it hosts its own regular litter pick-ups around Lee County.
  • It hosts a reading list of books on neighborhood, place, and household life, with room for our own conversation about each one.
  • It runs a mutual-aid board: a directory of pantries, warming centers, and family resources, plus a community board where neighbors can post needs and offers.

Who it's for

Local Lee is for anyone in Lee County who'd rather hear about the local restaurant down the street than another billion-dollar franchise extracting wealth from our community; for the farmer who finds independence growing their own seeds on their own land to sell to neighbors; or the parent looking for a babysitter who lives two blocks over.

You don't have to share any particular philosophy to be welcome here. If you'd like to know your neighbors and the businesses they keep, Local Lee is your place.

Where I'm coming from

I started Local Lee because I think a county is only as strong as the people who actually live in it. Not the shareholders of a chain three states away. The guy who fixes your tractor. The woman who runs the food pantry. The family selling sweet corn at the end of the driveway in late August. That's who a place is. The rest is just real estate.

So the practice the site is built around is plain. Spend your money close to home where you can. Show up to the things your neighbors are putting on, whether that's the Petunia Festival in June, a pancake breakfast at the fire department, or a work party out at Lowell Park. Pitch in when somebody needs a hand. The Leydig Center turns donated coats and old furniture into grants that go back into the county — that's roughly the shape of what I'm after.

People reach for big words for this kind of project: communitarian, agrarian, distributist, localist. Pick whichever one fits. I don't think you need any of them to know the name of the guy at the body shop in Amboy.

How it works

Anyone can submit a business, event, book, mutual-aid resource, or need-and-offer post. Each submission is read by an editor before it goes public. Posts on the needs-and-offers board automatically refresh every thirty days, so the board stays current without growing stale.

The county

Lee County sits in north-central Illinois along the Rock River, with Dixon — Reagan's hometown, and "Petunia City" to its neighbors — as the county seat. The Black Hawk War passed through here in 1832 and Abraham Lincoln served at Fort Dixon as a militia captain that summer; the Amboy Depot dates to the railroad era, and a band of restored tallgrass prairie at Nachusa Grasslands runs east of Franklin Grove with a small herd of bison on it.

We try to cover the whole of it: Dixon, Amboy, Ashton, Compton, Franklin Grove, Lee Center, Paw Paw, Sublette, West Brooklyn, Harmon, Nelson, Steward, and the farm country and unincorporated areas in between.
