About Local Lee

A neighborly network for the people of Lee County, Illinois.

What this is

Local Lee is a small, slow, locally minded website. It does four things:

  • It keeps a directory of locally owned businesses in Lee County — farms, shops, trades, eateries, and the rest. Owners can claim and update their own listing.
  • It runs a community calendar of events anyone in the county can submit.
  • It hosts a reading list of books on neighborhood, place, and household life, with room for our own conversation about each one.
  • It runs a mutual-aid board: a directory of pantries, warming centers, and family resources, plus a community board where neighbors can post needs and offers.

Who it's for

Local Lee is for anyone in Lee County who'd rather hear about the diner down the street than another franchise across the country — for the farmer who'd rather sell to a neighbor, the parent looking for a babysitter who lives two blocks over, the retiree with a workshop full of tools nobody is using.

You don't have to share any particular philosophy to be welcome here. If you'd like to know your neighbors and the businesses they keep, you're in the right place.

What we believe

That a place is its people; that a healthy economy starts at the kitchen table and the farm gate; that the work of belonging to a county is worth doing on purpose.

People reach for big words for this kind of project — communitarian, agrarian, distributist, localist. We don't mind any of them. But you don't have to use them. The practice is older than the labels: take care of the place you live and the people in it.

How it works

Anyone can submit a business, event, book, mutual-aid resource, or need-and-offer post. Each submission is read by an editor before it goes public. We're not interested in being a feed; we're interested in being a record. Posts on the needs-and-offers board automatically refresh every thirty days, so the board stays current without growing stale.

The county

Lee County, Illinois sits in the north of the state along the Rock River, with Dixon as the county seat. We do our best to cover the whole of it: Dixon, Amboy, Ashton, Compton, Franklin Grove, Lee Center, Paw Paw, Sublette, West Brooklyn, Harmon, Nelson, Steward, and the rural countryside that ties them together.

How to help

  • Add a business you know is locally owned.
  • Post an event happening in the county.
  • Suggest a book for the reading list.
  • Post a need or offer on the mutual-aid board.
  • Get in touch if you'd like to help with the editing.
